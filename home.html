Introduction

FURNACE BRAZE:

EMM ESS AIRCON have proven expertise in furnace / bright brazing of steel components (tubular & sheet metal) using Cu-filler alloys, and Brass / Cu components using Ag-filler alloys. We are pioneers in this specialized field, being the single source of many critical assemblies to OEs for over 15 - 20 years. We supply complete ready to assemble sub-assemblies. We have facility to manufacture input components like sheet metal and tube-parts.
EMM ESS AIRCON provides expertise in Furnace (bright H2–Cu) Brazing. This process involves brazing of two metallic surfaces, heating the parts in reducing atmosphere (H2), without requiring any flux. The filler alloy is applied at brazing areas. The assembly is heated to the melting temperature of the filler alloy, the filler alloy flows to all joining points, including hidden surfaces. This process gives superior, clean, bright brazing, causing minimal distortion & residual stresses in the parts. It requires no post treatments like normalizing or cleaning.
EMM ESS AIRCON is one of the leading manufacturers, and suppliers of tubing components of AC/Ref compressor-mufflers & accumulators, MPFI fuel delivery lines & fuel filters, bright brazing of SS parts for exhaust systems etc

Manufacturing Process

Step 1: Incoming Quality Control

Verify and inspect raw materials to ensure they meet required standards before use.

Step 2: Tube Forming

Shape metal or plastic tubes to desired dimensions using forming machines.

Step 3: Bending

Bend tubes or components to specified angles and shapes for further assembly.

Step 4: Child Parts Ultra-Sonic

Clean small component parts using ultrasonic waves to remove contaminants.

Step 5: Assembly Fitment

Assemble individual components accurately into sub-assemblies or final products.

Step 6: Cu Rings Insertion

Insert copper rings into assemblies for sealing, conductivity, or structural support.

Step 7: Furnace Brazing

Join metal components by heating and melting filler metal in a furnace environment.

Step 8: Inspection

Conduct visual and dimensional checks to confirm parts meet specifications.

Step 9: Final Bending

Apply precise bends to completed assemblies to meet final design geometry.

Step 10:Gauging

Measure parts using gauges to ensure dimensional accuracy and tolerances.

Step 11: Inspection

Perform final quality checks before packaging to ensure no defects remain.

Step 12: Delivery

Pack and ship finished products to customers or the next production stage.
